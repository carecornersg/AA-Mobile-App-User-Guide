    Notification Types

    üì¢ New Case Broadcast

    When: Upon creation of a new case and you are eligible based on matching criteria for the case

    What it shows:

    • Basic Case details (YS Gender, Age & Offence)
    • Broadcast type indicator (B1 (Blank)/B2üü°/B3üî¥)

    Action required from AA: Select accept if available, or close to decline.

    ‚úÖ Assignment Confirmation

    When: After you accept a case and are selected by the system.

    What it shows:

    • Full case details
    • Youth information
    • IO contact details
    • Full location with map

    Action required from AA: Confirm within 5 minutes or the case will be re-broadcasted to other AAs.

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Declining or timeout without response results in Low Priority flag for 14 days.

    ‚ùå Case Cancellation

    When: Investigation Officer cancels your assigned case by contacting AAYS hotline.

    What it shows:

    • Case ID
    • Cancellation reason
    • Option to submit documentation

    Action required from AA: Acknowledge within 30 minutes. Submit documentation if you've incurred travel expenses.

    ‚è∞ Interview Reminder

    When:Prompted via push notification 2 hours before your scheduled interview.

    What it shows:

    • Interview time and location
    • Case details

    Action required from AA: Prepare for the interview and commute accordingly.

    üìå Note: Interview reminders are always sent even if you have "Pause Notifications" enabled.

    üìÑ Documentation Reminder

    When: After interview is completed, if documentation not yet submitted 4 hours after interview start time.

    What it shows:

    • Case ID
    • Reminder to submit documentation

    Frequency: Sent every 4 hours

    Duration: Up to 24 hours (maximum 5 reminders)

    Escalation: After 24 hours, AAYS Staff will be notified

    Action required from AA: Go to My Assignments or Case Details and submit documentation accordingly.

    üìå Note: After 72 hours, AA will no longer be able to submit their documentation.This may lead to delays in disbursement of transport allowance.

    üèÜ Badge Achievement

    When: You earn a badge for your contributions.

    What it shows:

    • Badge name and details
    • Congratulations message

    When Will You Receive Case Broadcast?

    When a case is created, the system checks if you qualify based on 8 criteria. You must pass all stages to receive the notification:

    1. Qualifications ‚Äì Match case requirements (e.g., House Visit, Young Suspect Under 10)
    2. Region ‚Äì Your preferred activation region matches case location
    3. Timing ‚Äì Your timing preference matches interview time slot
    4. Gender ‚Äì Matches case requirement (if requested by IO)
    5. Language ‚Äì You speak required language (if requested by IO)
    6. Race ‚Äì Matches case requirement (if requested by IO)
    7. Cooldown ‚Äì No active case or case within 3 hours of interview start time
    8. Quiet Hours ‚Äì Not in pause/quiet hours mode

    Broadcast Types (B1, B2, B3)

    Indicator Broadcast Type Meaning
    No dot B1 (Standard) Broadcasted to primary activation region, based on weighted selection
    üü° Yellow B2 (Secondary) Broadcasted to primary + secondary region, based on First Come, First Serve
    üî¥ Red B3 (Staff-only) Urgent, all regions, based on First Come, First Serve

    B1: Standard Broadcast (No indicator)

    This is the first broadcast for a new case. Only AAs with primary region matching the case location will receive this notification.

    How it works:

    • Holding Period (5 mins): System collects all responses, then uses weighted selection
    • Waiting Period (10 mins): FCFS (first to accept is assigned the case)

    üü° B2: Secondary Region Broadcast (Yellow dot)

    Sent when B1 fails (no one responded/confirmed) or for non-urgent case re-broadcasts. AAs with primary OR secondary region matching case location will receive this.

    How it works:

    • No holding period ‚Äì FCFS immediately
    • First to accept is moved to confirmation

    üî¥ B3: Staff-Only Broadcast (Red dot)

    Sent for urgent cases (interview ‚â§ 4 hours away) after B2 fails, or for last minute cases (interview < 2 hours away). Only AAYS Staff receive this notification, ignoring all region/timing preferences.

    How it works:

    • No holding period - assigned to any AAYS staff that responds
    • AAYS Team also notified via email

    Low Priority Flag

    What is it?

    A Low Priority flag may be applied when:

    • You decline at the confirmation stage (after being selected) or timeout without response at the confirmation stage
    • You self-cancel an assignment

    Duration

    14 days from the decline action.

    Impact

    • Lower chance of being selected during weighted selection
    • Does not affect B2/B3 broadcasts
    • Does not prevent you from receiving broadcasts

    How to Avoid

    • Only accept cases you can truly attend
    • Check your calendar before accepting
    • Consider travel time and location
    • Confirm promptly when selected

      • üìå Note: If you have a valid reason for the cancellation, please inform AAYS team. Low priority flag can be removed on a case-by-case basis.

    Troubleshooting

    Not receiving notifications?

    • Phone notifications: Settings ‚Üí Apps ‚Üí CAS App ‚Üí Enable notifications
    • App permissions: Allow notifications in app permissions
    • Do Not Disturb: Ensure phone is not in DND mode

    Not receiving case broadcasts?

    • Quiet Hours / Scheduled Availability: Are you outside your configured time range for today?
    • Date Pause: Are you within your muted date range?
    • Cooldown: Do you have an active case or recent case within 3 hours?
    • Profile settings: Have you indicated your preferred region and timing correctly?
